SCOUT User Manual

Introduction

SCOUT is a powerful tool that allows you to upload data in CSV format, cluster the data according to individual distance information, and visualize the results.

Getting Started

Step 1: Uploading Your CSV File

  1. Locate the section titled "Upload your CSV file" at the top left of the SCOUT interface.
  2. Click on the input field and a dialog box will open.
  3. Navigate to the location of your CSV file on your computer.
  4. Select the file and click `Open` to upload it into the SCOUT system.

Clustering Data

Step 2: Choosing Clustering Options

  1. Locate the middle section titled "Choose Clustering".
  2. Click on the "Add Data" button to add data for clustering.
  3. Use the "Select CSV-Divider" option to select specific dividers within your CSV file.
  4. Choose the appropriate "Data Type" and "Scaling Algorithm" for your data from the dropdown menus.

Flag Matching

Step 3: Running Flag Match

  1. Locate the bottom section titled "Flag Match".
  2. Use the "Flag Check" button to check flags in your data.
  3. If necessary, use the "Delete All" button to remove all entries.
  4. Click on the "Run" button to start the flag match process.
  5. Click here to learn more about flags.
  • name: Does not affect data analysis. Make it easier to identify each entry in the cluster. Not required.
  • distance information: Flags for eculidean distance. Please make sure that each dimension in the coordinates is labeled as distance. Required feilds.
  • numerical flags: Flags for quantity features, will involved in detailed distribution analysisi with clusters. Choose as needed.
  • non-numerical flags: Flags for other features, will involved in detailed distribution analysisi with clusters. Choose as needed.

Remember, SCOUT is a powerful tool, but the quality of the results depends heavily on the appropriateness of the distance measure and visualization type for your specific dataset. Always ensure your choices align with your data and the insights you hope to gain.

Enjoy exploring your data with SCOUT!
